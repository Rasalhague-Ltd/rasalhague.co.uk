The Problem

What’s actually happening

A small problem can escalate quickly. A tenant sends a message about a leak: a photo and one line of text, delivered the way most issues are reported now. No one replies. The message slips up the thread and out of sight.

By the next evening, the water has reached the wiring and is running from the power sockets. Six people are still living in the house, still paying rent, and still waiting for help.

This is not an extreme example. It is an ordinary one — and I know because I lived inside it.

This is how UK shared housing often operates: a 650,000-property sector held together by chat threads, unanswered messages, and tenants chasing into silence.

Lived Experience

Four tenancies. The same failure, every time.

Four shared properties in Newcastle. Stays with friends in Manchester and London. Across agency-managed, private, and portfolio landlords. The pattern was identical:

  • 01Mould blamed on tenants and cats.
  • 02A leaking sewer no one repaired.
  • 03£2,000 lost to faulty heating equipment — still affecting my credit score today.
  • 04An attempted break-in to a tenant’s room. Door forced.
  • 05A heating system that smelled like rotten eggs. Fire service called.
  • 06A broken fence fixed with the broken pieces, six times over.

The buildings weren’t broken. The operating systems were. No reporting. No triage. No timelines. No proof of completion. No consequence for delay.

What It Is

Not an admin tool. An accountability system.

Rasalhague is the operational governance layer for UK shared housing — built on one idea: every issue is owned, timed, and proven.

Landlord and tenant are both first-class in the same loop. The tenant isn’t a name on a file. They report, watch the issue get logged and prioritised, and see it closed with proof.

Issues are raised through structured reports, ranked by the system rather than by mood, and given a defined response window. Closure requires evidence. The works log is permanent and cannot be edited.

No chat threads. No —we’ll get to it.— No mould blamed on the cats.

How It Works

Three steps. No chat threads.

  • 01 —Report. Tenants raise issues through structured forms — not messages that get lost.
  • 02 —Prioritise. Every issue is logged, ranked, and given a defined response timeframe. The system decides — not mood, not memory.
  • 03 —Resolve & prove. Closure requires evidence. The works log is permanent and cannot be edited.
